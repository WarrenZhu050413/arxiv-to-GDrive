Advancing Large Language Models: Systematic Benchmarking and Performance Analysis
Alex Researcher, Taylor Scientist, Morgan Developer
AI Research Institute

Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing, demonstrating impressive capabilities in understanding and generating human language. In this paper, we present a comprehensive analysis of state-of-the-art LLMs, evaluating their performance across multiple domains including factual knowledge, reasoning, code generation, and multilingual applications. We introduce a new benchmarking framework that provides more nuanced insights into model strengths and limitations. Our findings reveal that while recent models have made significant advances in logical reasoning and factual accuracy, challenges persist in maintaining coherence for complex, multi-step tasks and in handling domain-specific knowledge. We also analyze computational efficiency and propose strategies for optimizing inference without compromising output quality. This work contributes to the ongoing development of more capable, reliable, and efficient language models.
